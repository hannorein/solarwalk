Solar Walk

A new installation on the UTSC campus commemorates the 150th anniversary of Canada's confederation with a scale model of the solar system. The placement of the markers reflects the exact locations of the celestial objects on July 1, 1867.

Events on Canada Day Weekend

We welcome you to join us for a series of inaugural events on Canda Day Weekend. There will be guided tours with stops at planet markers on campus, followed by a talk by an astrophysicist, and public observing with the 30cm telescope at UTSC. The events will take place rain or shine, observing with the telescope is of course weather dependent.

  • Friday June 30th & Sunday July 2nd:
    Dr Alan Jackson - 150 Years of Solar System Astronomy
  • Saturday July 1st & Monday July 3rd:
    Dr Daniel Tamayo - Dance of the Planets: A Tale of Order and Chaos
The schedule on all four days will be as follows:
  • 7:30pm
    Solar Walk guided tour
    Starting point: bus loop, near parking lot A (Uranus), see map
  • 8:30pm
    Astrophysics talk by UTSC postdoctoral fellows and faculty
  • 9:30pm
    Public observing with the 30cm UTSC telescope
All events are suitable for the entire family. The walking tour and the talk are accessible. The UTSC observatory is located on the roof of the Science Wing building which is only accessible by stairs.

The project is supported by the UofT Scarborough 150 Fund.

Statement of Acknowledgement of Traditional Land

We wish to acknowledge this land on which the University of Toronto operates. For thousands of years it has been the traditional land of the Huron-Wendat, the Seneca, and most recently, the Mississaugas of the Credit River. Today, this meeting place is still the home to many Indigenous people from across Turtle Island and we are grateful to have the opportunity to work on this land.
